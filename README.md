### Short Bio

Svetlana “Lana” Yarosh is an Associate Professor in the [Computer Science & Engineering Department](http://www.cs.umn.edu/index.php) at [University of Minnesota](http://www1.umn.edu/twincities/index.html). Her research in [HCI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human%E2%80%93computer_interaction) focuses on [embodied interaction](http://www.dourish.com/embodied/) in [social computing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_computing) systems. Lana is currently most proud of graduating her first batch of [amazing PhD students](#students), being a writer for [CrashCourse AI](https://z.umn.edu/ccai), and being recognized for her contributions to undergraduate education with a [Horace T. Morse Award](https://scholarswalk.umn.edu/faculty-awards/awards-for-outstanding-contributions/morse-recipients). Lana has two Bachelors of Science from [University of Maryland](http://www.umd.edu/) (in Computer Science and Psychology), a Ph.D. in [Human-Centered Computing](http://hcc.cc.gatech.edu/) from [Georgia Institute of Technology](http://www.gatech.edu/), and two years of industry research experience with [AT&T Labs Research](http://www.research.att.com/).

### About My Research Group

I run the ProDUCT (**Pro**totyping, **D**esign, and **U**sability of **C**ommunication **T**echnologies) group. As part of the [GroupLens Research Center](https://grouplens.org/), we are committed to the design and empirical study of social computing systems, with my group frequently adding an embodiment perspective to these investigations (e.g., [adding projection and haptics to communication systems](https://lanayarosh.com/2017/11/squeezebands-hugging-through-the-screen/)). We do work in a variety of contexts, with particularly notable contributions in the areas of Child-Computer Interaction (e.g., [supporting intergenerational mentorship in schools](https://grouplens.org/blog/learning-from-learning-buddies-opportunities-for-tech-to-connect-across-generations/)), Online Health Communities (e.g., [examining social support on CaringBridge.org](https://grouplens.org/blog/take-your-medicine-write-online/)), and Pervasive Health (e.g., [helping people in recovery from substance use disorders get connected with support](https://grouplens.org/blog/enlisting-recovering-addicts-as-tech-designers-to-fight-relapse/)). Methodologically, we run the gamut from pure qualitative to mixed methods to design-based approaches, though our recent directions frequently include participatory design as an element as well.
